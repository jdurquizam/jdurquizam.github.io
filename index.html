I'm tropical forest ecologist and researcher specializing in geospatial ecology and remote sensing. My work primarily focuses on the Amazon basin, where he studies how extreme weather events and climate change affect forest dynamics, tree mortality, and greenhouse gas emissions.

Current Affiliations Max Planck Institute for Biogeochemistry (MPI-BGC) in Jena, Germany: He has been working here as a researcher (progressing from a Ph.D. researcher to a Postdoctoral role) within the Department of Biogeochemical Processes. Universidad Nacional de la Amazonía Peruana (UNAP) in Peru: He holds a position as a researcher and professor, where he previously coordinated the Applied Ecology and GIS Center.
